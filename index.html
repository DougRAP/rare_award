What Makes Someone R.A.R.E.?

The R.A.R.E. Award isn't about performance metrics or project completions. It's about character ‚Äî the extraordinary acts of service, integrity, and empathy that go well beyond normal expectations.

Exceptional Integrity

Demonstrating moral courage and personal sacrifice for the greater good, even when no one is watching.

Deep Compassion

Acting as a stabilizing force within the team through genuine empathy, especially during moments of crisis.

Unwavering Commitment

Quietly taking initiative to improve the culture, morale, and mission of RAP, often under great personal challenge.

Excellence in the Face of Hardship

"True strength is revealed in hardship. Those who continue to lift others even while burdened themselves reflect the deepest spirit of RAP."

Special consideration is given when an employee shows excellence under adversity ‚Äî continuing to act with empathy, discipline, and professionalism while navigating serious personal challenges outside of work.

Recognition Criteria

The R.A.R.E. Award is granted only when truly exceptional character is demonstrated

All employees are eligible, regardless of role or tenure. The award focuses on character and exceptional acts of service rather than job performance or seniority.
Recipients receive the equivalent of one month's base salary, capped at $5,000. This is processed through payroll in the next cycle following the announcement.
Up to once per quarter, at the sole discretion of the Chief Executive Officer. If no individual meets the required standard of exceptionalism in a given quarter, the award may not be issued. It is never guaranteed.
Qualifying actions include voluntarily helping others in moments of crisis, demonstrating moral courage or personal sacrifice, acting as a stabilizing force through compassion, supporting others while managing personal hardships, or quietly taking initiative to improve RAP's culture and mission.
The CEO may receive informal nominations or personal observations. Each potential recipient is reviewed confidentially with HR to ensure alignment with company standards. Upon approval, the CEO personally notifies the recipient and prepares a public recognition statement.
