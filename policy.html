1

Purpose

The R.A.R.E. Award honors the highest expression of RAP's culture — when an employee demonstrates exceptional integrity, compassion, and commitment, going far beyond expectations to support both the company and fellow employees.

Important Note:

It is not a performance award; it is a character award. It exists to recognize those rare individuals whose actions, often unseen or under great personal challenge, embody the very best of who we are.

2

Frequency & Award

Frequency

Up to once per quarter

Award Amount

One month's base salary (max $5,000)

Selection

CEO's sole discretion

Eligibility

All employees, any role or tenure

Note: If no individual meets the required standard of exceptionalism in a given quarter, the award may not be issued.

3

Definition of "Exceptionalism"

Exceptionalism reflects extraordinary acts of service, integrity, and empathy that go well beyond normal expectations. Recipients often distinguish themselves by:

Voluntarily helping others in moments of crisis or high pressure

Demonstrating moral courage or personal sacrifice for the greater good

Acting as a stabilizing force within the team through compassion and consistency

Quietly taking initiative to improve the culture, morale, or mission of RAP

4

Exceptionalism in the Face of Hardship

"True strength is revealed in hardship. Those who continue to lift others even while burdened themselves reflect the deepest spirit of RAP."

Special consideration is given when an employee shows excellence under adversity — continuing to act with empathy, discipline, and professionalism while navigating serious personal challenges outside of work.

Examples may include:

  • • Supporting others while managing a family illness or personal loss
  • • Remaining positive and dependable during difficult personal or financial circumstances
  • • Upholding RAP's values under extraordinary stress or uncertainty
5

Selection Process

1

Nominations & Observations

The CEO may receive informal nominations or personal observations from any source within the organization.

2

Confidential Review

Each potential recipient is reviewed confidentially with HR to ensure alignment with company standards and policy compliance.

3

CEO Decision & Notification

Upon approval, the CEO personally notifies the recipient and prepares a public recognition statement.

6

Presentation & Recognition

Recognition Elements

Public presentation at company meeting
Formal certificate of recognition
Permanent record on company intranet
Bonus payment in next payroll cycle

Hall of Excellence

All recipients are permanently recognized on the company recognition board under the heading:

"Those Who Made RAP R.A.R.E."

7

Confidentiality & Consent

Because R.A.R.E. stories often involve personal or sensitive circumstances, recipients may choose how much of their story is shared.

The CEO and HR will coordinate all communications with full respect for privacy and dignity.

8

Program Integrity

The R.A.R.E. Standard

The R.A.R.E. Award is never guaranteed. It exists to preserve the principle that true excellence — moral, not mechanical — is rare.

No quota, rotation, or nomination count determines it. It is granted only when the CEO determines that an act or individual genuinely represents the spirit of RAP at its best.
